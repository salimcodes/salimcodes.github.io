Hey. Hi. I am Salim Oyinlola.

Hey. Hi; Welcome to my website!

I am an Undergraduate student in the Department of Electrical and Electronics Engineering at the University of Lagos. An IEEE Computer Society Richard E. Merwin Scholar, I am affilated with the University of Lagos' Artificial Intelligence and Robotics Laboratory under Dr. Chika Yinka-Banjo whilst working under Prof. Peter Olabisi Oluseyi from the department of Electrical and Electronics Engineering as my undergraduate thesis supervisor. My research question is around how I am developing and applying machine learning algorithms for intelligent decision-making and predictive modeling in real-world power and energy systems and agriculture.

Per my understanding of the importance of telling stories, I tell stories of students doing amazing things with their careers in a bid to inspire others with the Beyond the Classroom platform.

In addition to my academic activities, I am actively involved in tech communities and I constantly share my knowledge and experience in AI, IoT, Cloud technologies - particularly Microsoft Azure, and GitHub for open source. I also serve as a GitHub Campus Expert and Microsoft Learn Student Ambassador in my school. Furthermore, I author technical articles on everything between Artificial Intelligence/Machine Learning and Cloud Computing. In that regard, I try to approach technical writing as a story-telling experience.

Also, in line with my passion for making impact, I champion a mental health advocacy group named SoundAsABell Initiative. The initiative, which addresses the third Sustainable Development Goal [Good Health and Well-Being, is geared towards educating young Nigerians about mental illnesses in a bid to alleviate the stigma and stereotypes associated with persons suffering from mental illnesses. Most recently, we partnered with the Lagos State Government for a campaign to empower secondary school students in the state to understand, talk about, and seek help for mental health issues i.e. breaking the culture of silence and shame from an early age.

Non-work-wise, I follow football, the NBA, and Formula 1. When I am not binge-watching some random sitcom, I try to add to my bank of "useless" knowledge by reading autobiographies of as many people as I can draw inspiration from. I could also be writing for fun.
